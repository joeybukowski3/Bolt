📋 Methodology

How serial number dates are decoded and products are researched.

How Serial Number Decoding Works

Manufacturers embed the production date directly inside the serial number using a proprietary format. The "How Old is my Item?" tool applies known, published format tables for each brand to extract the year (and in many cases the week or month) of manufacture from the serial string.

The decode logic runs entirely in your browser — no serial number is sent to any server during a standard decode. The results are instant and private.

Format Sources

Decoding rules are sourced from manufacturer technical documentation, authorized service publications, and cross-referenced community databases. Each format is verified before inclusion. Supported categories include:

  • Appliances: Samsung, Whirlpool, Maytag, KitchenAid, Amana, GE, LG, Frigidaire, Electrolux, Kenmore, Bosch, and more
  • HVAC: Carrier, Bryant, Lennox, Trane, American Standard, Goodman, York, and more
  • Water Heaters: Rheem, Ruud, Bradford White, A.O. Smith, State Industries, Reliance, and more
  • Electronics: Apple, ASUS, Google Pixel, and select Sony products

AI-Assisted Item Lookup

When no serial number is available, the Item Lookup feature uses an AI language model (Google Gemini) to research a product based on the brand, model number, and description provided. The AI returns structured data including production era, estimated age, MSRP, depreciation, replacement options, and technical notes.

Results are estimates drawn from general product knowledge and publicly available information. They are not decode outputs and carry a wider margin of uncertainty than serial-based lookups.

🔍 Real-Time Verification

After the initial AI research pass, a second verification call is made to confirm the current market status of the product (in production, discontinued, or available as a refurbished/used item). This two-pass approach reduces the chance of stale or rumored information appearing in results.

Confidence Levels

High: Single unambiguous date extracted from a known serial number format.

Medium: Multiple possible years due to repeating manufacturer cycles (e.g., a 10-year code cycle). The most likely year is shown with alternatives noted.

Low / Estimated: AI-assisted result, incomplete serial data, or brand/model without a known decode format. Treat these as starting points for further research.

Valuation Methodology

Actual Cash Value (ACV) estimates are calculated using the formula: ACV = MSRP × (1 − depreciation rate × age). Depreciation rates are sourced from category norms and manufacturer service-life data. Users can adjust the assumed age to recalculate ACV for their specific unit. All valuations are estimates and should be verified against current market pricing at point of settlement.
